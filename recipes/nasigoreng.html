Nasi Goreng

Indonesia's Famous Fried Rice

Prep: 20 mins Cook: 10 mins Serves: 2-3 Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked white rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 shallots, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 red chilies, sliced (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp shrimp paste (terasi)
  • 2 tbsp sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 1 tbsp regular soy sauce

Protein Options

  • 200g chicken, shrimp, or tofu (your choice)

Garnishes

  • 2 fried eggs (sunny side up)
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 2 tbsp fried shallots
  • Prawn crackers (krupuk)
  • Lime wedges

Cooking Instructions

  1. Prepare ingredients: Chop all vegetables and protein into bite-sized pieces. Break up cold rice with your hands to separate grains.
  2. Make the paste: In a mortar, grind together garlic, shallots, chilies, and shrimp paste until it forms a coarse paste.
  3. Cook protein: Heat 1 tbsp oil in wok over high heat. Cook your chosen protein until done. Remove and set aside.
  4. Fry the paste: Add remaining oil to wok. Fry the spice paste for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add rice: Add rice to wok, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry for 2 minutes.
  6. Season: Add both soy sauces and mix thoroughly. Return cooked protein to wok and combine.
  7. Serve: Plate the rice topped with fried egg, cucumber slices, fried shallots, and prawn crackers. Serve with lime wedge.

Indonesian Cooking Tips

  • Use day-old rice for best texture - fresh rice makes mushy nasi goreng
  • Traditional kecap manis (sweet soy sauce) is essential for authentic flavor
  • Cook on highest heat possible for proper "wok hei" (breath of the wok)
  • For vegetarian version, omit shrimp paste and use mushroom sauce instead

Popular Variations

Nasi Goreng Kampung

Village-style with salted fish and green beans

Nasi Goreng Seafood

With shrimp, squid, and mussels

Nasi Goreng Petai

With stinky beans for adventurous eaters
